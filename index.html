02 / 17 Overview

Executive Summary

IC Candle Co. is a direct-to-consumer candle brand powered by AI and fulfilled through print-on-demand. We sell custom, personalized candles online with zero inventory and near-zero operating costs.

Mission

To brighten homes with clean-burning, thoughtfully curated scents that provide a moment of peace — delivered through a modern, AI-driven e-commerce experience with zero waste and zero inventory risk.

The Model

Two revenue streams powered by Printify's multi-vendor platform. Stream 1: Our curated IC Candle Co. branded line across multiple vendors. Stream 2: Custom candle service — customers submit imagery, we AI-style it (caricatures, comic book, cartoon, superhero, niche themes) into unique labels. All orders auto-route through Printify. AI handles all marketing — replacing $11K–19K/mo in agency costs.

Core Values

Sustainability, transparency in ingredients, premium quality. All candles use plant-based wax, phthalate-free fragrance, and recyclable vessels.

Key Advantage

4-person team, each with domain expertise. AI replaces an entire marketing department. We launch for ~$1,185 total (~$296/person) and break even at just 30 candle sales.

Market Analysis

The global scented candle market is growing, with a strong shift towards sustainability and wellness-focused aromas. Consumers increasingly seek clean-burning, non-toxic options with premium ingredients.

Target Market

Individuals aged 25–40, primarily homeowners or apartment dwellers who prioritize interior décor and self-care routines. Eco-conscious professionals seeking premium, personalized products.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike mass-market brands, IC Candle Co. offers unique, artisanal scent profiles with premium non-toxic wax blends — delivered via a lean POD model that eliminates inventory risk and enables rapid iteration.

17 / 17 Risk

Risk Management

⚠️

Chargeback Risk

You are the seller of record. If a customer disputes a charge, YOU are named first — not the supplier. Document every order with tracking number, fulfillment confirmation, and customer communications. Chargebacks without documentation are almost always lost.

↩️

Returns

  • Suppliers do NOT accept returns on POD items
  • Damaged/wrong → replace, no return required
  • Customer changed mind → store credit or exchange
  • Personalized items → final sale (state on listing)
  • Budget 2–3% of revenue as returns buffer
🛡️

Insurance

  • You carry exposure as the visible brand
  • Get General + Product Liability (~$300–600/yr)
  • Providers: Next Insurance, Thimble, Hiscox
  • Read supplier terms — know where liability ends
  • Get coverage BEFORE your first sale
📋

Record Keeping

  • Log every order: tracking #, fulfillment date, destination
  • Screenshot customer comms if issues arise
  • Use Printify dashboard for all order tracking
  • Records are your defense in any payment dispute

Pipeline Steps

  1. Order Detection — Backend polls Etsy every 5 minutes for new paid orders
  2. Photo Download — Grabs the buyer's uploaded photo from the order data
  3. Image Processing — Resizes and crops to exact label dimensions for the selected jar
  4. Upload to Printify — Sends the processed label image to Printify's media library

The Printify Liability Gap

Critical

As a POD seller, you are 100% legally liable for product safety even though Printify manufactures and ships the candles. Courts hold the seller of record responsible regardless of the supply chain.

Printify’s Merchant Protection is extremely limited:

  • Only covers reprints and refunds for defective products
  • Will not cover legal defense, settlements, or injury claims
  • Will not cover property damage caused by a candle defect
  • Their terms explicitly state they are “not responsible for any losses you incur”

Candle-specific risks include burns from hot wax, house fires from container cracks or defective wicks, allergic reactions to fragrance oils, and respiratory issues from strong scent emissions. Industry standard coverage is $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate.

Shopify Cyber Liability Inflection Point

When you move to Shopify (Phase 3), the data exposure profile changes:

  • You become a data controller under privacy law
  • Shopify stores customer data (names, addresses, order history) on their servers
  • Shopify handles PCI compliance for payment processing
  • But your account security is your responsibility—if phished or compromised, the breach is on you
  • Customer data becomes exportable (CSV downloads create local exposure)

Decision-Making Framework

AOR Decisions

Each founder has final say within their area of responsibility. No group vote needed.

Examples: Ricardo chooses design tools, Robert picks insurance provider, Jose sets listing schedules, Mark adjusts pricing within approved ranges.

Creative & Strategic

Require majority approval (3 of 4). These shape the brand direction and cannot be unilateral.

Examples: Brand name changes, new product lines, marketing strategy pivots, major partnerships, target audience shifts.

Financial Commitments

Expenses over budget require majority approval. Day-to-day expenses within budget are AOR decisions.

Examples: New tool subscriptions over $50/mo, ad spend increases beyond approved budget, one-time purchases over $200.

Task Ownership Protocol

  1. Tasks are assigned by AOR in the Tasks tab. Each founder is responsible for their column.
  2. Each founder reviews their tasks daily before the morning standup.
  3. Setting a due date = signing off on ownership of that task. A green “✓ Signed” badge will appear. Tasks without due dates show an amber warning.
  4. If you cannot own a task, do not sign off. Instead, flag it as a concern (⚠) so it gets discussed at standup. The task will turn red.
  5. Flagged concerns and blocked tasks are first priority at every daily standup.
  6. Once signed off, you are accountable for delivering by the due date. If the timeline slips, flag it early—don’t wait until it’s overdue.
Key Principle

Communicate early, communicate often. It is always better to flag a concern than to silently miss a deadline. No one will be faulted for raising issues—only for hiding them.

Daily Morning Standup

Format (15 min max)

  • Before the meeting: Review your tasks. Update statuses. Flag any concerns.
  • Each founder reports: What’s done, what’s in progress, any concerns flagged.
  • Red flags first: Flagged concerns and blocked tasks are discussed immediately.
  • Reassignment: If a task can’t stay with its current owner, agree on a new owner during standup.
  • No deep dives: Complex discussions get scheduled separately. Standup stays focused.
